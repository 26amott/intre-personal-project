Interpersonal Relationship Analysis

Using the Johari Window Model

By: Abigail Mott

Introduction

Interpersonal communication plays an important role in how relationships develop and change over time. One theory that explains self-awareness in relationships is the Johari Window model. This model shows what is known and unknown between individuals in a relationship. It can help explain how communication, self-disclosure, and perception affect the development of relationships over time (Felt & Packer, 2023).

Johari Window

Johari Window Model

Open Area

Known to self and others

Information known by both myself and others. This includes traits, behaviors, and experiences that are openly shared in a relationship.

Blind Area

Unknown to self, known to others

Information that others see about me that I may not be aware of, such as habits or behaviors that impact the relationship.

Hidden Area

Known to self, unknown to others

Information I choose not to share, such as personal thoughts or experiences that remain private early in a relationship.

Unknown Area

Unknown to both

Information unknown to both myself and others, including traits or abilities that have not yet been discovered.

Relationship 1: My Mom

Beginning

Since my mom is my biological mother, we technically met at my birth. However, our relationship didn’t really start developing until I was old enough to think, communicate, and understand things better.

Development

As I grew up, my relationship with my mom changed as I was developing as a person. My opinions started to differ from hers, which sometimes led to arguments or disagreements. A lot of that came from me growing and trying to figure things out. But as I’ve gotten older, I’ve been able to communicate better with her, which has resulted in a really strong and healthy relationship.

Johari Window Analysis

  • Open Area: A lot of my life has always been open to my mom, like my interests, personality, hobbies, and goals. She probably knows the most about me out of anyone because she’s been there the whole time.
  • Hidden Area: Earlier in my life, I kept more things to myself because I wasn’t sure how my thoughts or feelings would be received. Over time, I’ve become more comfortable sharing things, so my hidden area has gotten smaller.
  • Blind Area: My mom is really good at noticing things about me that I don’t realize myself. She’ll point out behaviors or reactions that I don’t see, which has helped me become more self-aware (perception checking).
  • Unknown Area: As I’ve grown, both my mom and I have discovered new things about me that we didn’t know before. Because of how long we’ve known each other, we’ve been able to figure out a lot of those unknowns together.

Communication Skills

Communication has always been a big part of my household. We explain what we’re doing and what’s going on, which helps everyone stay on the same page. I’ve learned how to communicate with my mom effectively by using "I" statements and explaining my perspective. She also shows active listening, which helps a lot during conversations. This kind of communication keeps things from turning into bigger problems (Felt & Packer, 2023).

Current Status

Right now, my relationship with my mom is strong and maintained every day. I’ve grown to respect her more, which has helped keep things positive between us. She helps me with a lot in my life, and I maintain the relationship by communicating, respecting her, and helping her when I can.

Relationship 2: Close Friend (No Longer Friends)

Beginning

This relationship started when I met her at swim. We got along pretty quickly and started talking a lot during practice, which eventually turned into hanging out outside of swim too.

Development

For about the first year and a half, the friendship was good. We spent a lot of time together and got along well. But over time, differences in our goals, beliefs, and priorities started to show. At first it wasn’t a big deal, but it slowly built up and made things harder. One example of this was that she was very controlling. If I wanted to hang out with other friends or do something else, she would get upset and question why I wasn’t spending that time with her. Because of that, I started to feel like I couldn’t be completely honest or speak up about what I wanted, which made the relationship more stressful. Eventually, it got to the point where I felt like continuing the friendship didn’t really make sense anymore, which led to me ending it. That ended up causing a lot of drama.

Johari Window Analysis

  • Open Area: At the beginning, we shared a lot about ourselves, like interests and experiences, which helped us become close.
  • Hidden Area: As things started to feel off, I kept more of my thoughts to myself instead of saying them, which made my hidden area bigger.
  • Blind Area: There were probably things I was doing that I didn’t realize were affecting the friendship (like how I acted sometimes). Those blind spots likely contributed to the tension.
  • Unknown Area: Over time, we both started realizing things about each other that we didn’t see at first, especially differences in values. That’s what made it clear we weren’t as compatible as we originally thought.

Communication Skills

Communication played a big role in how this relationship turned out. Toward the end of the friendship, I felt like I couldn’t speak up honestly because of how she would react, which made communication less effective. There were times where better perception checking could have helped avoid misunderstandings, but instead assumptions were made. I tried to stay respectful and use "I" statements instead of escalating things, but not everything was handled perfectly. Active listening could have improved things on both sides. Overall, differences in communication made the situation worse over time (Felt & Packer, 2023).

Current Status

The friendship has ended, and we don’t really communicate anymore besides small talk if we have to. After it ended, there was some drama and negative talk, but I chose not to respond the same way. Ending the friendship allowed me to focus on relationships that align better with my goals and values.

Relationship 3: Coworker Turned Friend

Beginning

This relationship started at work, where we talked mainly because we had to. It was more of a situational friendship at first since we were working together.

Development

Over time, we started actually enjoying talking to each other and began hanging out outside of work. For example, we went to get food together after a shift, which helped shift the relationship from just coworkers to actual friends. After that, we started texting and even FaceTiming sometimes, which showed that the relationship was growing beyond just work. Even though we got closer, it didn’t reach the same level as some of my other friendships. The relationship is still somewhat dependent on work, and we don’t feel a strong need to maintain it outside of that environment.

Johari Window Analysis

  • Open Area: As we became friends, we started sharing more about our lives, which increased the open area between us.
  • Hidden Area: There are still things I keep to myself, since the relationship isn’t as deep as others. So my hidden area is still somewhat large.
  • Blind Area: Since they don’t know me as well as some other people do, they aren’t able to notice as many things about me that I might be unaware of.
  • Unknown Area: There hasn’t been as much opportunity to discover unknown aspects of myself in this relationship, since it hasn’t developed to that level yet.

Communication Skills

Communication in this relationship is pretty relaxed and straightforward. We use basic communication skills like listening and responding, and conversations feel natural. As we’ve shared more about our lives, the open area of the Johari Window has increased through self-disclosure. However, since the relationship is not as deep, it hasn’t required more advanced communication like conflict resolution. Because of that, the relationship stays simple and easy to maintain (Felt & Packer, 2023).

Current Status

Currently, we are still friends and coworkers. The relationship is maintained through regular interaction at work and occasional time spent outside of work. It’s a more casual friendship, but still positive.

Conclusion

Applying the Johari Window model helped me better understand how self-awareness and communication impact my relationships over time.
